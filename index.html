What Are The Three Pillars of ESG?

ESG investing evaluates companies on more than just their bottom line. It's a holistic approach that uses three key pillars to measure a company's sustainability and ethical impact.

The Ratings Puzzle

A major challenge in ESG is that rating agencies use different methods. This means the same company can get very different scores, making direct comparisons difficult. It's crucial for investors to look beyond a single score.

Beware of "Greenwashing"

Greenwashing is when a company or fund makes misleading claims about its ESG credentials. As an investor, it's vital to do your own research and watch for these common red flags.

Vague Language

Look out for fuzzy terms like "eco-friendly" or "sustainable" that aren't backed up by specific data or actions.

Misleading Names

A fund might have "green" or "social" in its name, but its actual holdings may not align with those principles.

Lack of Transparency

Genuine ESG-focused companies and funds provide detailed reports on their strategies and progress. A lack of data is a major red flag.

Irrelevant Claims

Highlighting a minor positive ESG action to distract from significant negative impacts in other areas of the business.
